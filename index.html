Early life and reasons for a math major

Born on May 15, 1998 in Saigon Vietnam. I immigranted to the United States when I was 4 years old under a family visa. Staying with my Father,Mother, Brother and 30 other family members in one house in Vista, California. Growing up with little to nothing and family expectations of being great, I focused on education and strived to be the best student I could be. However, life wasn't without it's own difficulties. Having the last name HO and being the only asian kid was a challenge in school all the bullying seen in school became a reality and school-life wasn't as simple as going to school and studying. It became about surviving the mockery, the physical/verbal abuse, and the expectations of the family. Throughout my years in school, I found solice in numbers. Numbers never lie, mock or abuse. Numbers had rules and a system that it follows.

Work towards Goal

I found myself as an undergraduate for Appiled Mathematics at UCR. Knowing I had much to learn, I focused in class and have a gpa of 3.35. I strove to learn more and joined the Math club. However, learning math wasn't enough, the real world demanded more. It demanded I learn how to code. I struggled at first, but I learned to overcome and am now proficient in C++, Java, Python, Matlab, R, SAS, STAT, SQL, and HTML. This however only made me antisocial and I lacked communication skills and experience. I found a job at a coffee shop and learned how to interact with people and learned how to work in a system, under stress and a clock, and gave me experience in a professional environment. I took this experience and tranferred it over to my internship at Raytheon and where I found more holes in myself and worked towards a verison of me that was competent and able to excel in all aspect of a workplace environment.
